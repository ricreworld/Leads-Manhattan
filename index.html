The signal ladder

Six moments before a door opens

Ranked from earliest warning to too-late. The ones marked LIVE are wired into the feed below right now. The ones marked MANUAL are real, free, and worth checking by hand, but nobody publishes a clean API for them.

01

Community board liquor notice

NY law requires 30 days' written notice to the local community board before filing an on-premise liquor license application. It's public record, discussed at CB meetings, and almost nobody outside the neighborhood reads it.

Manual watch

02

Alt-CO filing submitted, before approval

DOB NOW classifies any filing that changes a building's use, occupancy, or egress as an Alt-CO. That classification means someone is legally converting a space into a different kind of business, and it shows up the moment the filing is submitted, months before a permit is even approved.

Live below
90–240 days outDOB NOW Job Filings
02b

Construction permit approved

DOB approves the permit before a single wall gets built out. This is the confirmation step after the Alt-CO filing above, and it's also where general construction permits for simpler renovations show up.

Live below
45–180 days outDOB NOW Approved Permits
03

Liquor license application posted

The State Liquor Authority publishes active licenses daily, including temporary operating permits, which is how many new spots open their doors while the full license is pending. A fresh filing at a Manhattan address means a bar, restaurant, or retail liquor operation is actively moving toward opening.

Live below
30–90 days outNYS Liquor Authority
04

Signage permit filed

Exterior signage needs a DOB sign permit. It usually lands close to the finish line, but it's a hard confirmation that the buildout is nearly done.

Manual watch
14–30 days outDOB NOW sign filings
05

First health inspection filed, not yet conducted

DOHMH flags a brand-new food establishment with a placeholder inspection date before the actual visit happens. It means the health permit is in hand and the doors are close.

The honest answer

Can you actually build this?

YES

This is a real, buildable tool — and you're looking at a working version of it

The three live feeds above are genuine public APIs with no cost and no login. What you're looking at right now already does the core job: it surfaces Manhattan addresses that are actively becoming something, before that something opens its doors.

The honest limitation is coverage, not cost. The earliest signal on the ladder, community board notice, has no API; it lives in PDFs on volunteer-run websites. That one stays a five-minute manual habit, not a feed. Everything else here runs unattended.

Realistic path: use this page for a few weeks and get a feel for the noise level in the permit feed, since general construction permits include renovations that aren't new tenants. Once you know what a good lead looks like, the next step is a lightweight script that runs weekly, filters harder, and drops results straight into a Google Sheet or your Salesforce import queue.
